Executive Summary: To classify fraud in real-time, raw transaction fields must be translated into high-impact behavioral vectors [10]. Our engineered features capture temporal patterns, transaction velocity anomalies, and device metadata changes that standard rule engines fail to correlate.
Key Engineered Behavioral Features:
is_round_amount (Boolean): Flagging highly structured round payments (e.g., exactly ₹5,000, ₹10,000, ₹50,000) which frequently occur in social engineering, extortion, and OTP scam campaigns.
amount_to_average_ratio (Float): Computes current transaction amount divided by the user's historical 30-day average. Extreme spikes indicate account compromise.
days_since_last_transaction (Float): Analyzes dormant accounts that suddenly become hyper-active, which is typical of compromised or bought mule accounts.
transactions_last_day (Int): Captures rapid transaction velocity or "layering" (multiple fast payments to drain a balance).
device_change_flag (Boolean): Evaluates device handshakes. If the system detects a different hardware profile while an active call is flagged, risk surges [5, 6].
